Key Insights - Knowledge Management

Knowledge Management (KM) plays a vital role in the sustainability of non-profits like Edinburgh Tabernacle. By leveraging the SECI model—Socialisation, Externalisation, Combination, and Internalisation—we can better capture, share, and utilise the collective knowledge within our church. Effective KM helps us maintain continuity, even when there are changes in our volunteer or staff members and supports strategic decision-making.

Recommendations

  • Formalise Documentation Processes: Develop clear and accessible documentation for all church processes. This will ensure continuity and help new volunteers and staff quickly understand their roles.
  • Encourage Knowledge Sharing: Implement regular meetings or workshops where staff and volunteers can share ideas and experiences. This encourages a culture of continuous learning and improvement.
  • Invest in Training Programs: Provide ongoing training to ensure that everyone understands the importance of KM and how to effectively manage and share knowledge within the church.

Key Insights - Data Privacy

Data privacy is more critical than ever, especially with regulations like GDPR that impose strict requirements on how we handle personal data. Our church, like many non-profits, faces challenges in ensuring that sensitive information is protected. Issues such as limited resources, inconsistent data management practices, and the need for ongoing staff and volunteer training were identified as key concerns.

Recommendations

  • Enhance Data Security Measures: Implement stronger data security protocols, including encryption and role-based access controls, to protect sensitive information.
  • Conduct Regular Audits: Schedule regular audits of our data management practices to ensure compliance with GDPR and other regulations.
  • Promote Data Privacy Awareness: Launch awareness campaigns and training sessions for staff and volunteers to keep them informed about best practices in data protection.

Integration of Technology

To support both KM and data privacy, it is recommended that the church invest in advanced technological tools, in line with our budget. Tools such as secure cloud storage, encrypted communication platforms, and knowledge management software can greatly boost our efficiency and security.
