About the Project

In our increasingly digital world, managing knowledge and safeguarding data privacy have become essential for non-profit organisations, including church charities like Edinburgh Tabernacle.

This project stems from my dissertation, which focused on developing a comprehensive framework to address these needs within our church. By effectively managing our knowledge and protecting personal data, we can enhance our operational efficiency, build trust within our community, and comply with legal obligations.

Objectives

  • Identify the specific challenges our church faces in managing and protecting data.
  • Explore best practices for knowledge management (KM) and data privacy tailored to non-profits.
  • Develop a framework that integrates KM and data privacy to improve our church's operations and security.

    • Methodology

    To understand our church's current practices and challenges, I employed a qualitative research approach. Through semi-structured interviews with our church's administrative staff, I drew insight. The analysis was guided by two key frameworks: the SECI model of knowledge creation and the Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) framework. These tools provided a structured approach to understanding how we can improve both KM and data privacy.
